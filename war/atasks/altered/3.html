WOLF HOLLOW

Rating: 4.23
Lauren Wolk
VERDICT: BUY IT
  • "Trusting its readers implicitly with its moral complexity, Wolk's novel stuns. (Historical fiction. 9-13)"
  • Evil comes to rural Pennsylvania in an unlikely guise in this novel of the American homefront during World War II.

RISING STAR: THE MAKING OF BARACK OBAMA

Rating: 3.88
David J. Garrow
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "Too long by half but consistently readablean impressive work that will provide grist for the former president's detractors and admirers alike."
  • An exhaustive epic of Barack Obama's trajectory to the presidency.

JACKIE'S GIRL: MY LIFE WITH THE KENNEDY FAMILY

Rating: 4.25
Kathy McKeon
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "McKeon's delightful memories have been tucked away for 50 years, and thankfully, she has brought them out to share the enchanting magic of Camelot with us all."
  • In her first book, McKeon recounts her years of working for Jacqueline Kennedy.
Available In Audiobook

DEMOCRACY: STORIES FROM THE LONG ROAD TO FREEDOM

Rating: 3.82
Condoleezza Rice
VERDICT: BUY IT
  • "Some readers may not be convinced, but this book deserves a broad audience, especially in our current political climate."
  • George W. Bush's secretary of state returns to her academic roots with this accessibly written study of that imperfect but ideal form of government.

A DOG'S WAY HOME

Rating: 4.13
W. Bruce Cameron
VERDICT: SKIP IT
  • "More Disney than drama."
  • In Cameron's (Puppy Tales: A Dog's Purpose Collection, 2016, etc.) sentimental tale, a stray puppy finds a loving home, loses it after running afoul of anti-pit-bull regulations, and then, after being sent into exile, makes a wilderness trek through the Rocky Mountains home to Denver.
Available In Audiobook

MEN WITHOUT WOMEN

Rating: 3.79
Haruki Murakami
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "Not groundbreaking but certainly vintage Murakami: a little arch, a little tired, but always elegant."
  • "Our relationship isn't exactlynormal": as ever, a glimpse into the strange worlds people invent by the always inventive Murakami (Absolutely on Music: Conversations, 2016, etc.).

SINCE WE FELL

Rating: 3.73
Dennis Lehane
VERDICT: BUY IT
  • What seems at the start to be an edgy psychological mystery seamlessly transforms into a crafty, ingenious tale of murder and deceptionand a deeply resonant account of one woman's effort to heal deep wounds that don't easily show."
  • Don't zoom through this latest entry in Lehane's illustrious body of work. You'll miss plenty of intrigue, intricacies, and emotional subtleties.

THE THIRST

Rating: 4.03
Jo Nesb
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "Middling for this distinguished series: yet more evidence of why Scandinavian crime writers continue to dominate international bestseller lists."
  • Retired Inspector Harry Hole, who thinks he's safe from his demons as an underpaid lecturer in Oslo's Police College, gets blackmailed into returning to the Crime Squad Unit, with predictably explosive results.

DRAGONS LOVE TACOS 2: THE SEQUEL

Rating: 3.64
Adam Rubin
VERDICT: BORROW IT
    "Most young readers, even those in diapers, would agree. (Picture book. 3-5)"
  • As if the fiery disaster of the first outing (2012) weren't bad enough, news that the world's entire supply of tacos has been used up leaves not just the dragons dismayed.

YOU DON'T LOOK YOUR AGE: ...AND OTHER FAIRY TALES

Rating: 3.83
Sheila Nevins
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "As in many collections, some of the pieces are disposable, but the best ones are honest, opinionated, and spirited."
  • A miscellany of musings about aging, love, work, and wisdom.

ASTROPHYSICS FOR PEOPLE IN A HURRY

Rating: 3.74
Neil DeGrasse Tyson
VERDICT: BUY IT
  • "A sublime introduction to some of the most exciting ideas in astrophysics that will leave readers wanting more."
  • America's most approachable astrophysicist distills the past, present, and (theoretical) future of the cosmos into a quick and thoroughly enjoyable read for a general audience.

ROBERT B. PARKER'S LITTLE WHITE LIES

Rating: 4.06
Ace Atkins
VERDICT: SKIP IT
  • "Readers taken in by Atkins' sureness of touch in the first half of this schizoid yarn richly deserve to get flimflammed by the bait-and-switch that follows."
  • A damsel in distress enlists Boston's most storied private eye in her cause and then has second and third thoughts.

THE BROKEN ROAD

Rating: 4.05
Richard Paul Evans
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "A thoughtful, well-plotted yarn that will evoke either pity or schadenfreude."
  • In a prologue, novelist Evans (The Mistletoe Secret, 2016, etc.) recounts meeting a (fictional) man in a restaurant on Route 66 in the Mojave Desert who has hiked the famous highway all the way from Chicago.

INTO THE WATER

Rating: 3.98
Paula Hawkins
VERDICT: SKIP IT
  • "Let's call it sophomore slump and hope for better things."
  • Women in a small British town have been drowning since 1679.

JUST FLY AWAY

Rating: 3.80
Andrew McCarthy
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "A poignant, character-driven coming-of-age novel that, despite a too-tidy ending, will appeal broadly to teen readers. (Fiction. 14-18)"
  • Fifteen-year-old Lucy's world is rocked when her father confesses to her and her sister that they have a half brother, the result of a brief affair.
Available In Audiobook

THE UNBREAKABLE CODE

Rating: 4.63
Jennifer Chambliss Bertman
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "Brisk, bookish good fun for puzzle and code lovers. (historical afterword) (Mystery. 9-13)"
  • Riddles in an online book-finding game put young cipher sleuths Emily and James on the trail of both buried treasure and a vengeful arsonist.

CLIMATE OF HOPE: HOW CITIES, BUSINESSES, AND CITIZENS CAN SAVE THE PLANET

Rating: 3.73
Michael Bloomberg, Carl Pope
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "Whether this is an exercise in thinking globally and acting locally or vice versa, a thoughtful, eminently reasonable set of proposals for saving New Yorkand therefore the world."
  • Just in time for Earth Day, yes, a hopeful book of strategies for delivering the planet from our worst environmental depredations.
Available In Audiobook

THE SECRETS OF MY LIFE

Rating: 3.97
Caitlyn Jenner, Buzz Bissinger
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "Painting a life both shallow and deep, painstakingly choreographed and unscripted, Jenner's candid portrait of a self in the remaking is a marvel to behold."
  • A rugged tale of brute beauty and pyrrhic individualism.

THE OPERATOR: FIRING THE SHOTS THAT KILLED OSAMA BIN LADEN AND MY YEARS AS A SEAL TEAM WARRIOR

Rating: 4.13
Robert O'Neill
VERDICT: SKIP IT
  • "A fast-paced account quite likely to engender strong reactions among readers concerned with the U.S. military's roles in foreign conflicts."
  • A war memoir from a highly decorated Navy SEAL.
Available In Audiobook

OPTION B: FACING ADVERSITY, BUILDING RESILIENCE, AND FINDING JOY

Rating: 3.71
Sheryl Sandberg, Adam Grant
VERDICT: BUY IT
  • "A book that provides illuminating ways to make headway through the days when there doesn't seem to be a way forward."
  • A memoir of the loss of a husband and finding a path forward beyond the grieving process.
Available In Audiobook

BEARTOWN

Rating: 4.20
Fredrik Backman
VERDICT: BORROW IT
  • "A thoroughly empathetic examination of the fragile human spirit, Backman's latest will resonate a long time."
  • In Beartown, where the people are as "tough as the forest, as hard as the ice," the star player on the beloved hockey team is accused of rape, and the town turns upon itself.

ANYTHING IS POSSIBLE

Rating: 3.99
Elizabeth Strout
VERDICT: BUY IT
  • "Another powerful examination of painfully human ambiguities and ambivalencesthis gifted writer just keeps getting better."
  • A radiant collection of stories linked to Strout's previous novel, My Name Is Lucy Barton (2016, etc.), but moving beyond its first-person narration to limn small-town life from multiple perspectives.
Available In Audiobook

TOUCH THE EARTH

Rating: 4.04
Julian Lennon, Bart Davis
VERDICT: SKIP IT
  • "'It's time to head back home,' the narrator concludes. 'You've touched the Earth in so many ways.' Who knew it would be so easy to clean the place up and give everyone a drink? (Picture book. 6-8)"
  • A pro bono Twinkie of a book invites readers to fly off in a magic plane to bring clean water to our planet's oceans, deserts, and brown children.
